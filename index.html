The Coming AI & Humanoid Robot Economic Shock

A public-facing pamphlet and an institutional-investor report on the economic and labor-market impact of AI and humanoid robots over the next 10 years.

Something big is about to happen to the job market. Over the next 10 years, AI software and humanoid robots will be able to do a lot of the work people currently do. Companies are planning to produce millions of humanoid robots. AI is already replacing parts of office, legal, coding, and analyst work.

This research asks the right questions in plain language: which jobs go first, how fast, who gets hurt most, what it costs governments, what asset markets are missing, and what policy changes could actually prevent the worst outcomes. Both documents are free to read and share.

Our estimate: 250 million to 1.6 billion jobs globally over 10 years.

What makes this research different?

Coupled feedback-loop simulation (no variable is modeled in isolation), calibrated to the Anthropic Economic Index (2025), audited by three independent large language models. Full methodology transparent in the institutional report.

How to use the leading indicators

The research publishes specific predictions at 3, 6, 12, and 18 months for recent-graduate unemployment, humanoid-robot deployments, and software-engineering job postings. Check the public sources (NY Fed, IFR, LinkedIn, Challenger) and see whether reality tracks our predictions or the mainstream consensus.
